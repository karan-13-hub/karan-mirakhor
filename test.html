About Me

I am a PhD student in Computer Science with a passion for advancing the field of Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning. My research focuses on developing efficient algorithms for coordination and planning under uncertainty, with applications in robotics, autonomous systems, and distributed decision-making.

I am particularly interested in zero-shot coordination, where agents must collaborate with previously unseen partners, and how we can leverage offline policy diversity and online belief reasoning to achieve this goal. My work combines theoretical foundations with practical implementations to solve real-world challenges.

Publications

Efficient Zero-Shot Coordination via Offline Policy Diversity and Online Belief Reasoning

A central challenge in multi-agent reinforcement learning is zero-shot coordination (ZSC): the ability of agents to collaborate with previously unseen partners. We propose a novel approach that combines offline policy diversity with online belief reasoning to achieve efficient coordination without prior training with specific partners.

ICLR 2026 Multi-Agent RL Zero-Shot Coordination
2026 Co-authored View Paper

Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning for Autonomous Vehicle Coordination

This work presents a novel multi-agent reinforcement learning framework for coordinating autonomous vehicles in urban environments. Our approach addresses the challenges of partial observability and dynamic traffic conditions through a hierarchical learning architecture.

ICRA 2025 Autonomous Vehicles Hierarchical RL
2025 Lead Author View Paper

Decentralized Multi-Agent Planning with Communication Constraints

We investigate the problem of decentralized multi-agent planning under communication constraints. Our approach uses a novel message-passing algorithm that enables effective coordination while respecting bandwidth limitations and communication delays.
